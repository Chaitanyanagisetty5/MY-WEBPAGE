Manufacturing Process

From Sand to Silicon

Every DDR3 chip starts as SiO₂ in beach sand. Drag to scroll the complete semiconductor journey — 6 stages from raw material to packaged ASIC die.

Stage 01 — Extraction
Silicon Dioxide (SiO₂)
Quartz sand — 99.7% SiO₂ — is reduced with carbon at 1600 °C to produce metallurgical-grade silicon, the foundation of all VLSI devices.
SiO₂ + 2C → Si + 2CO · MGS ~98% pure
Stage 02 — Purification
Electronic-Grade Polysilicon
Trichlorosilane (SiHCl₃) is decomposed at 1100 °C in the Siemens CVD process, depositing ultra-pure polysilicon rods — 11-nines purity.
SiHCl₃ + H₂ → Si + 3HCl · 11N purity
Stage 03 — Crystal Growth
Czochralski Crystal Ingot
A seed crystal is slowly pulled from a 1414 °C silicon melt, rotating to grow a single-crystal cylindrical ingot 300 mm in diameter and up to 2 m long.
300mm dia · CZ process · <100> orientation
Stage 04 — Wafering
Silicon Wafer
Diamond wire saws slice the ingot into 775 μm-thick wafers. Chemical mechanical planarization (CMP) achieves sub-nanometer surface flatness for lithography.
300mm · 0.775mm thick · Ra < 0.1nm
Stage 05 — Lithography
EUV Lithography
13.5 nm extreme ultraviolet light exposes patterns on photoresist through a reticle mask. Repeated for ~100 layers to build transistors, vias, and metal interconnects.
EUV · 13.5nm wavelength · 5nm node
Stage 06 — Packaging
Packaged ASIC
The die is bonded to a substrate with gold bond wires or flip-chip bumps, encapsulated in epoxy mold compound. BGA solder balls form the final PCB interface.
BGA · flip-chip · 400 MHz · DDR3 CTRL

HBM3 UVM Verification IP

JEDEC HBM3 · 16 Independent Channels · RAL · IEEE-1500 · DBI/ECC/Parity

A fully self-developed HBM3 UVM VIP that I architected and implemented from scratch, leading a 5-member team. The environment instantiates 16 independent channel agents (each with driver, monitor, sequencer, and scoreboard), a global agent, an IEEE-1500 boundary-scan agent, and a virtual sequencer that coordinates all channels simultaneously.

The unique FSM + Shared Memory synchroniser enforces JEDEC command-to-command timing between the channel monitor and channel driver across all 16 channels. The interface models true HBM3 bidirectional buses: DQ[16][63:0], DBI[16][7:0], ECC/SEV, DPAR, APAR with tri-state direction control. Clock generation implements the JEDEC PLL ÷2 relationship: CK = WDQS/2.

Covers 30+ command sequences (ACT, WR/WRA, RD/RDA, PREPB/PREAB, REFAB/REFPB, RFMAB/RFMPB, SRE/SRX, PDE/PDX, NOP/CNOP/RNOP, MRS) and 25+ test scenarios including seamless/non-seamless burst, power-down retention, self-refresh, DBI+parity, ECC, tFAW compliance, and per-bank refresh.

AXI3 UVM Testbench

Single Master · Single Slave · Full 5-Channel AXI3 Coverage

A complete UVM-based verification environment for the AXI3 protocol — covering all five channels: Write Address (AW), Write Data (W), Write Response (B), Read Address (AR), and Read Data (R). Implements out-of-order and overlapping transaction modes via plusargs, constrained-random stimulus, functional coverage groups, and SVA protocol assertions.

Architecture includes: layered sequences (base → write-only → read-only → write-then-read → parallel), master/slave agents with active/passive modes, scoreboard with in-order and out-of-order checking, coverage collector, and assertion module connected directly to the interface clocking block.
